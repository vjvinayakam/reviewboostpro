Why Your Google Rating Dropped — And How to Fix It

RB
ReviewBoost Team
April 2026
8 min read

You checked your Google Business Profile this morning and something felt off. Your rating dropped. Maybe from 4.6 to 4.3. Maybe from 4.1 to 3.8. Or maybe your review count went down — reviews you earned over years are simply gone.

This is one of the most panic-inducing moments for any Indian business owner. And it's happening more often than ever in 2026, as Google's review moderation systems have become significantly more aggressive.

The good news: most of these drops are fixable. But you need to diagnose the right cause first — because the fix for "reviews disappeared" is very different from the fix for "a bad review tanked my average."

Here are the 7 real reasons your Google rating drops — and exactly what to do about each one.

📊 What's happening in 2026 Google's AI-powered review moderation became dramatically more aggressive in 2025–2026. Review deletion rates increased by over 400% compared to 2024. Even genuine, legitimate 5-star reviews are being removed at record rates — often without any explanation to business owners.

The 7 Reasons Your Google Rating Dropped

1
Google deleted your reviews
This is the most common reason in 2026. Google's AI now removes reviews it suspects are inauthentic — and it makes a lot of false positives. If several customers reviewed you from the same WiFi network (like a clinic waiting room), or if multiple reviews came in on the same day, Google's algorithm may flag them as suspicious and remove them. Even completely genuine 5-star reviews disappear this way.
✅ Fix: Check your review count in Google Business Profile vs what shows publicly. If they differ, report it at support.google.com/business → "Reviews" → "Missing reviews." Document which reviews are missing with screenshots. Some can be reinstated through the appeal process.
2
A new bad review dropped your average
The maths are brutal when you have few reviews. If you have 20 reviews averaging 4.5 stars and one person leaves a 1-star review, your rating drops to 4.3 immediately. With only 10 reviews, a single 1-star review can drop you by 0.4 stars. This is the most common reason for small clinics and shops to see sudden drops.
✅ Fix: The fastest solution is dilution — get more genuine 5-star reviews quickly. Five new 5-star reviews will recover most of the lost average. Start sending WhatsApp review requests to recent happy customers today. Also respond professionally to the bad review publicly.
3
A reviewer deleted or edited their review
Customers can edit or delete their own Google reviews at any time. If someone who left you a 5-star review is no longer happy with your business, they may go back and change it to a 1-star. Or they might delete it entirely. Google doesn't notify you when this happens — you just see your rating change.
✅ Fix: Unfortunately you can't prevent this. The best protection is volume — with 80+ reviews, one changed review barely moves your average. Focus on consistently collecting new reviews so individual changes have less impact.
4
Your Google Business Profile was suspended or flagged
If Google suspects your profile violates its guidelines — keyword stuffing in your business name, suspicious activity, an address issue, or a complaint from a competitor — it may suspend your profile. A suspended profile often shows a much lower rating or may not appear in Maps at all.
✅ Fix: Check your Google Business Profile dashboard for any warnings or suspension notices. If suspended, go to business.google.com/appeals and submit a reinstatement request with proof of your business (GST certificate, shop photos, utility bill). Most genuine businesses are reinstated within 7–14 days.
5
Competitor fake review attack
In competitive markets — especially busy areas of Indian cities where multiple clinics, salons, or restaurants compete on the same street — competitors sometimes hire services to post fake 1-star reviews. You might see a sudden cluster of 1 and 2-star reviews from accounts with no profile photos and no other review history, often posted within a short time window.
✅ Fix: Report each suspicious review via Google Maps (three dots → "Report review" → "Spam or fake"). Use the Google Business Profile Review Management Tool to track status. File a bulk report at support.google.com/business explaining the pattern. Document everything with screenshots. See our full guide on removing fake reviews.
6
Review velocity looked suspicious to Google
If you ran a one-time campaign and got 30 reviews in 3 days — even from real customers — Google's algorithm can flag this as suspicious and remove many of them. Sudden spikes in review volume trigger automated moderation regardless of whether the reviews are genuine. This often happens to businesses that run "leave a review and get a discount" campaigns.
✅ Fix: Never run mass review campaigns. Instead, send requests consistently — 3 to 5 per day, every day. Steady velocity looks natural to Google. Also, never offer incentives for reviews — this violates Google's policies and will get reviews removed.
7
The reviewer's Google account was deleted
When a customer deletes their Google account, all their reviews across all businesses are automatically deleted too. This is completely out of your control and has nothing to do with anything you did. It happens silently and you have no way to recover those reviews.
✅ Fix: Nothing you can do to recover these specific reviews. The only protection is volume — if you have 100 reviews and 5 accounts are deleted, your rating barely moves. Keep collecting consistently.

How to Diagnose Which Reason Is Yours

Before doing anything, figure out exactly what happened:

1
Check your review count — compare what shows publicly on Google Maps vs what shows in your Google Business Profile dashboard. If they differ, Google removed some reviews.
2
Look at recent reviews — did any new negative reviews appear recently? Screenshot your full review list sorted by "newest" and check for anything suspicious.
3
Check for suspicious patterns — multiple 1-star reviews from accounts with no photos, no other reviews, created recently = likely fake attack.
4
Check your profile status — log into business.google.com and look for any warnings, suspension notices, or verification requests.
5
Calculate the math — if your count is the same but rating dropped, a customer edited their review or you got a new bad one. Sort reviews by "newest" to find it.

The Fastest Way to Recover Your Rating

Regardless of the cause, the fastest path to recovery is the same: get more genuine 5-star reviews as quickly as possible.

Here's the maths. If you have 30 reviews at 4.0 stars (total points = 120) and you get 20 new 5-star reviews, your new average is 140 + 100 = 240 points ÷ 50 reviews = 4.8 stars.

Volume is your best defence against every type of rating drop — deleted reviews, bad reviews, edited reviews, fake attacks. The more genuine reviews you have, the more resilient your rating is to any individual change.

400%
Increase in Google review deletions in 2025–2026. Businesses with fewer than 30 reviews are most vulnerable — one deletion can move the rating by 0.2+ stars.

What NOT to Do When Your Rating Drops

❌ Never do these Don't buy fake 5-star reviews to compensate — Google detects them and will remove them, often triggering a penalty that makes things worse

Don't respond aggressively to bad reviews — it looks unprofessional and pushes future customers away

Don't create multiple Google accounts to leave yourself reviews — this is policy violation and grounds for permanent suspension

Don't offer discounts or gifts in exchange for reviews — Google's AI detects incentivised review patterns and removes them

Don't ignore the drop and hope it fixes itself — without action, it won't

How to Protect Your Rating Going Forward

The businesses whose ratings survive all of this — deleted reviews, fake attacks, bad reviews — share one thing in common: they collect reviews consistently, every single week.

"When Google deleted 12 of our reviews in October 2025, our rating dropped from 4.6 to 4.2 overnight. We panicked. But because we had a system sending WhatsApp review requests daily, we recovered to 4.7 within 3 weeks. Volume saved us." — Auto repair shop owner, Pune

A clinic with 8 reviews is devastated by one deletion. A clinic with 150 reviews barely notices. The goal is to build a review count large enough that no single event — a deletion, a bad review, a competitor attack — can meaningfully move your rating.

✅ Your rating protection checklist Weekly: Send WhatsApp review requests to everyone who visited that week
Monthly: Check your review count in GBP dashboard vs public view
Monthly: Reply to all new reviews — positive and negative
Quarterly: Screenshot your full review list in case you need evidence later
Always: Never incentivise reviews, never buy reviews, never ask multiple times
