Dr. Priya Sharma had been running SmileCare Dental in Chennai's T. Nagar neighbourhood for six years. Her patients loved her. Her retention rate was excellent. But when new patients searched "dental clinic near me" or "dentist in T. Nagar", SmileCare didn't show up.

The clinic was stuck at position 7 on Google Maps — invisible to the 70% of patients who only look at the top 3 results. With 14 Google reviews averaging 4.1 stars, SmileCare simply didn't have the prominence to compete with newer clinics that had actively collected reviews.

Dr. Sharma didn't have a quality problem. She had a visibility problem.

Why Email Wasn't Working

Before ReviewBoostPRO, Dr. Sharma's receptionist would occasionally send review request emails to patients. The results were disappointing — less than 5% of patients ever clicked the link, and even fewer actually left a review.

The problem wasn't the request — it was the channel. In India, most patients don't check email regularly. But they check WhatsApp dozens of times a day. A review request sitting in an inbox for three days gets ignored. A WhatsApp message gets seen within minutes.

The Exact System They Used

SmileCare's workflow was simple. After every patient visit, the receptionist would open ReviewBoostPRO, enter the patient's name and WhatsApp number, and tap "Open in WhatsApp & Send." The whole process took about 10 seconds per patient.

WhatsApp would open with this pre-filled message:

📱 WhatsApp message sent to every patient
Hi Kavitha 👋

Thank you for visiting SmileCare Dental today! We hope your treatment went smoothly.

Could you spare 30 seconds to share your experience? It means the world to us and helps other patients in Chennai find trusted care. 🙏

👉 g.page/smilecare-dental/review

When a patient tapped the link, they first saw a 1-5 star rating screen. If they selected 4 or 5 stars, they were immediately redirected to Google to leave a public review. If they selected 1-3 stars, they were sent to a private feedback form instead — their concern went directly to Dr. Sharma's inbox, never reaching Google publicly.

The Private Feedback Advantage

One of the most valuable outcomes wasn't the reviews themselves — it was the private feedback system. During the 60 days, SmileCare received 8 private feedback submissions from unhappy patients (1-3 star ratings).

Without ReviewBoostPRO, all 8 of those would likely have become public 1 or 2-star Google reviews. Instead, Dr. Sharma resolved 6 of them directly. Two of those resolved patients went on to leave 5-star public reviews after their concerns were addressed.

What Made the Difference

Dr. Sharma attributes the results to three things:

  • Timing — sending the request within 2 hours of the appointment, while the experience is fresh
  • WhatsApp — using the channel patients already check constantly, not email
  • Consistency — doing it for every single patient, every single day — not as a one-time campaign
✅ Key takeaway SmileCare didn't change their service, their pricing, or their staff. They changed one thing — how they asked patients for reviews. The result was 6× more reviews, a higher rating, and a top 3 Google Maps position in 60 days.
