You open Google Maps one morning and see it — a 1-star review from someone you've never treated. No name you recognise. No details about a real visit. Just a vague complaint designed to damage your reputation.

It's one of the most infuriating things that can happen to a doctor. And unfortunately, it's more common than you think — especially from competitors, disgruntled ex-staff, or people who simply have the wrong clinic.

The good news: Google does remove fake reviews — if you follow the right process. This guide tells you exactly what to do, step by step.

First: Is It Actually Removable?

Before you start, you need to understand what Google will and won't remove. Many doctors waste time reporting reviews that Google won't act on — and miss the ones they actually could get removed.

✅ Google WILL remove
  • ✓Review from someone who never visited your clinic
  • ✓Fake review from a competitor's account
  • ✓Review containing hate speech or profanity
  • ✓Spam or promotional content in a review
  • ✓Review posted by a bot or fake account
  • ✓Review about a completely different business
  • ✓Extortion reviews (demanding money to remove)
❌ Google WON'T remove
  • ✗Genuine negative review — even if unfair
  • ✗Review you simply disagree with
  • ✗Exaggerated but real patient complaint
  • ✗Review from a patient you had a dispute with
  • ✗Low rating with no text (not a policy violation)
  • ✗Old reviews that hurt your average rating
⚠️ Important reality check Google does not mediate factual disputes. If a real patient genuinely had a bad experience and left a 1-star review, Google will not remove it — even if you believe it's unfair or exaggerated. Only reviews that clearly violate Google's policies can be removed.

How to Identify if a Review is Fake

Before reporting, gather evidence. Check these signs:

  • No match in your records — the name doesn't appear in your appointment book or patient records
  • Suspicious profile — the reviewer has 0 other reviews, was created recently, or has reviewed many different businesses in a short time
  • Generic language — "very bad service", "worst doctor" with no specific details about a real visit
  • Wrong details — mentions staff, services, or experiences that don't match your clinic
  • Pattern of attacks — multiple 1-star reviews appearing in a short period from different accounts
  • Same timing as a competitor opening nearby — common in Indian cities

Screenshot everything before you take any action. You'll need this evidence.

Step-by-Step: How to Report the Fake Review

1
Flag the review directly
Go to Google Maps → find your clinic → click the review → tap the three dots (⋮) → "Report review" → select the best matching reason (Spam, Fake engagement, Conflict of interest, Off topic, or Policy violation). This starts Google's process and creates a record.
2
Use the Reviews Management Tool
Go to business.google.com/reviews → find the review → click "Report review". This specialised tool is more effective than flagging from Maps. You can track the status of your report here.
3
Contact Google Business Profile support
Go to support.google.com/business → choose "Manage reviews" → "Report inappropriate reviews". Explain clearly: who the reviewer is, why you believe it's fake, and what evidence you have. Be factual, not emotional.
4
Respond to the review publicly
While you wait, always respond professionally. Your response is read by hundreds of future patients. A calm, factual response builds more trust than the fake review damages.
5
If rejected — appeal once
Google allows one re-evaluation. Go to Google Business Profile Help → "Contact us" → explain why the review violates policy. Provide screenshots, appointment records, or any evidence that proves the reviewer was never a patient.

How to Respond to a Fake Review Publicly

Always respond — even to fake reviews. Your response is not for the fake reviewer. It's for the thousands of future patients who will read it. Here's a template that works:

📋 Copy this response
Thank you for your feedback. We take all patient experiences very seriously. However, after a thorough review of our records, we are unable to identify a patient by this name or any appointment matching the experience described. We are committed to providing the highest standard of care to every patient. If this review was intended for our clinic, we welcome you to contact us directly at [phone number] so we can understand and address your concern. — Dr. [Name], [Clinic Name]
💡 Why this response works It's calm and professional. It clearly signals the review may be fake without being accusatory. It shows future patients that you are transparent and responsive. And it invites real resolution — which a fake reviewer will never take up.

If It's a Competitor Attack — Escalate

If you suspect a competitor is behind multiple fake reviews (common in Indian cities where clinics compete in the same neighbourhood), take these additional steps:

  • Document the pattern — screenshot all suspicious reviews, their profiles, and dates
  • Use Google's Merchant Extortion form — if someone is demanding money or favours in exchange for removing reviews, this is illegal. Report at support.google.com using the extortion-specific form
  • Report the reviewer profiles — flag each suspicious profile on Google Maps as fake
  • Contact a lawyer — if the fake reviews contain defamatory claims, a cease and desist letter often makes reviewers back down immediately
🚨 Never do this Never respond aggressively or accusatorially in public. Never try to pay someone to remove a review. Never create fake positive reviews to counter the fakes. All of these violate Google's policies and can get your Business Profile suspended entirely.

The Best Long-Term Defence: More Real Reviews

Here's the truth about fake reviews that most doctors don't realise: a single fake 1-star review matters much less when you have 80 real 5-star reviews.

A clinic with 4.7 stars from 90 reviews and one recent 1-star fake is far more credible than a clinic with 4.9 from 8 reviews. Volume and consistency of real reviews is your best protection.

How Long Does Google Take to Remove a Review?

Typically 3 to 10 business days for an initial response. If Google finds the review violates policy, it's removed. If they don't act, you have one appeal. The entire process can take 2–4 weeks.

Some reviews sit in "pending" status for much longer, especially if the evidence isn't strong. This is why responding publicly and building more real reviews is equally important — you can't just wait for Google.
